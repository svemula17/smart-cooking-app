# App Name, Subtitle & Promo Text

## Primary name
**SmartCooking**

(30 chars max for Apple, 30 chars for Google Play; we're at 12 — room to grow if you ever rebrand.)

## Apple App Store subtitle (30 chars)
Pick one — both fit the limit:

- `Cook smarter, eat better` (24)
- `Plan • Cook • Track Macros` (26)
- `Your AI sous-chef` (17)

**Recommended:** `Cook smarter, eat better` — same tagline that appears on the login screen, gives the listing a consistent voice.

## Promotional text (Apple only, 170 chars, editable without re-review)

Used for in-the-moment promos — change it without a release:

> 273 recipes across 8 cuisines, household cook scheduling, macro tracking, and an AI chef that knows what's already in your pantry. (149 chars)

## Google Play short description (80 chars)
Limit is strict. One line. Tested options:

- `Plan meals, cook recipes, track macros — with an AI chef in your pocket.` (72)
- `273 recipes, 8 cuisines, AI chef, household coordination, macro tracking.` (73)

**Recommended:** the first one — verb-first, sounds like a benefit not a feature dump.

---

## Notes

- Apple shows the **subtitle** under the app name in search results — make it descriptive, not clever.
- Apple's **promo text** is the only field you can change without re-review. Use it for seasonal pushes ("New: Diwali recipes ✨").
- Google Play **short description** is shown above the screenshots; this is what someone reads before they decide to scroll.
